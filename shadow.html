29 Oct • 17 • Static

I Don’t Trust

I don’t trust people.
Not friends, not relationships. Every time I trusted someone, they left. So now I keep my distance. I don’t open up. I don’t rely on anyone. If I feel hurt or heavy, I shut down and disappear. It’s easier than getting attached and ending up disappointed.

I Feel Too Much

I feel things too strongly and it pisses me off. Small stuff affects me more than it should. If someone stops talking like before, my mind jumps to the conclusion that they don’t want me around. So I stop trying. I stop talking. I cut myself out before they do.

Friendships

Friendships don’t feel real anymore. People lose interest fast. They replace you the moment someone better shows up. If someone acts different, gives short replies, or starts ignoring me, I don’t ask questions. I just back out. I don’t beg, I don’t chase, I don’t explain. If they care, they’ll notice. They usually don’t.

Relationships

Relationships are the same. I don’t believe in “forever” or “loyalty.” People talk big, but they leave when they get bored. Words mean nothing without actions, and most people don’t have consistency. I’m not interested in someone who treats me like an option. If someone wants me, they show up. Simple.

I Get Attached

I get attached fast, but I never show it.
I care too much, but I act like I don’t.
I hate that about myself.

When It Gets Heavy

When life gets heavy — depression, anxiety, suicidal thoughts — I don’t reach out. I stay quiet. I isolate. I don’t tell anyone what I’m dealing with. Not because I’m strong, but because I don’t trust anyone to actually give a damn.

I Push Away

I push people away so they don’t get the chance to hurt me.
I distance myself before they change on me.
I prefer being alone because it’s predictable.

This Is Me

I’m not “fine.” I just don’t talk about it.
I’m not “cold.” I’m tired.
This is who I am.
